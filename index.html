Deeply fascinated by the natural world right from my childhood and my interest grew deeper and deeper over time. When I first stared into a microscope, I was transfixed with what I saw. I was captivated by the details of cell structure of an onion cell. Later while working with microorganisms, I realised that these microbes were too small to be detected from naked eye. What made it more interesting to me was the fact that several types of these microbes have not only been implicated in human disease but also in human welfare.
